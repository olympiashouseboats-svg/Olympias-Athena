Trip Planning

Don't Forget to Stay at Olympias Houseboat

The one line item Kashmir travelers most often regret leaving off their itinerary.

It happens more often than you'd think: a traveler builds a tight Kashmir itinerary around Gulmarg, Pahalgam, and Sonmarg, books a city hotel in Srinagar to save time, and only realizes on the drive to the airport that they never actually spent a night on Dal Lake. If there's one thing to build your schedule around, it's this.

Why It Gets Skipped

Houseboats sometimes get treated as a "nice to have" rather than a core stop, especially on shorter trips where Gulmarg and Pahalgam eat up most of the calendar. The mistake is treating a houseboat night as optional sightseeing instead of what it actually is — the most distinctly Kashmiri part of the whole trip.

How to Actually Fit It In

  • Bookend your trip with it. Arrive into Srinagar and spend your first or last night on Olympias before heading out to Gulmarg or Pahalgam — no detour required.
  • Book 2 nights, not 1, if your schedule allows. The first evening is arrival and settling in; the second is when the slower pace of lake life actually starts to land.
  • Confirm dates early in peak season. Spring (March–May) fills up 4–8 weeks out. Autumn and winter are far more flexible, often with same-week availability.
  • Let the houseboat replace a "sightseeing day," not add to it. A morning Shikara ride through the floating gardens covers as much of Srinagar's character as most guided city tours.

The honest version: almost nobody who stays on a well-run Dal Lake houseboat says they wish they'd skipped it. Plenty of people who skip it wish they hadn't. Build it in early, not as an afterthought.
