Understanding Olympias Athena: Why Two Houseboats Under One Name?

When guests first discover Olympias Athena, they often ask: "Are these two separate houseboats or one?" The answer: we operate two distinct premium houseboats — Olympias and Athena — both located at Boulevard Road, Ghat No. 13 on Dal Lake, both under the same trusted management, and both maintaining identical standards of excellence. This guide explains our unique structure and what it means for your Kashmir houseboat experience.

The Olympias Athena Story

Olympias Athena Group of Houseboats represents Kashmir houseboat excellence through two sister vessels — Olympias and Athena — each a masterpiece of traditional Kashmiri craftsmanship, each offering 8+ luxury rooms, and each maintained to the highest standards by owner Saif ul Rehman Guroo and his dedicated team.

🏛️ Why "Olympias Athena"?

The name combines classical Greek mythology with practical branding. Olympias was the mother of Alexander the Great, while Athena was the Greek goddess of wisdom and warfare — both powerful, regal names befitting premium accommodations. Using both names together creates a unified brand identity while honoring each houseboat's individual character.

🤝 One Management, Two Vessels

Both Olympias and Athena are:

  • Owned and operated by the same family (Saif ul Rehman Guroo)
  • Located at the same premium address (Boulevard Road, Ghat No. 13)
  • Maintained to identical quality standards
  • Staffed by the same trained hospitality team
  • Offering the same services (complimentary Shikara rides, authentic Wazwan cuisine, personalized care)
  • Priced comparably based on room category, not houseboat name

💡 What This Means for You

When you book with Olympias Athena, you're guaranteed premium quality regardless of whether Saif assigns you to Olympias or Athena houseboat. Both deliver the same exceptional experience — the assignment simply depends on availability and your group size. Think of it like booking a premium hotel chain with multiple buildings on the same property.

Why We Operate Two Houseboats

1. Increased Capacity Without Sacrificing Intimacy

A single large houseboat with 20+ rooms can feel impersonal — like a floating hotel. Two smaller houseboats (8-10 rooms each) maintain intimate, boutique atmosphere while collectively offering 15+ rooms to accommodate larger groups, families, or wedding parties.

2. Guaranteed Availability

When one houseboat is fully booked, the second provides backup. During peak season, this ensures we can accommodate more guests without compromising quality or turning away bookings.

3. Flexible Group Arrangements

Large families or friend groups traveling together can book an entire houseboat for privacy while still benefiting from our central management. Wedding groups or corporate retreats particularly appreciate this flexibility.

4. Maintenance Without Disruption

Houseboats require regular maintenance. Operating two vessels allows us to schedule repairs on one while continuing operations on the other — ensuring we never close and guests always have access.
