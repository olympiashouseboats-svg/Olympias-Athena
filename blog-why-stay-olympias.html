Travel Guide

Why You Need to Stay at Olympias Houseboat

Dal Lake has hundreds of houseboats. Here's what actually sets Olympias apart.

There are over 850 registered houseboats on Dal and Nigeen Lakes today, ranging from modest budget rooms to serious luxury. If you're planning a Kashmir trip, it's worth knowing what actually separates a memorable houseboat stay from a forgettable one — and why Olympias keeps coming up in that conversation.

1. The Location Is Genuinely Prime

Boulevard Road, Ghat No. 13 sits among the most established stretches of Dal Lake — sheltered water, easy Shikara access, and unobstructed views of the Zabarwan range. Not every houseboat on the lake can say that; some are moored in narrower, busier channels with far less privacy.

2. The Craftsmanship Isn't for Show

Kashmir's houseboat tradition runs on hand-carved cedar and walnut work passed down through generations of local artisans. At Olympias, that craftsmanship covers ceilings, bed frames, and window lattices — it's structural to the building, not a few decorative accents added for photos.

3. You're Dealing With the Owner, Not a Booking Agent

Saif ul Rehman Guroo has personally run Olympias for over 15 years. That matters more than it sounds — questions get answered by someone who actually knows the lake, the weather, the best Shikara routes, and your specific room, not a call center reading from a script.

4. Nothing Extra for the Shikara Rides

Many houseboats quote a room rate and then charge separately for every lake excursion. Olympias includes unlimited Shikara rides in the stay — floating gardens, sunset routes, local markets — without a running tab.

5. The Food Is the Real Thing

Authentic Wazwan, cooked fresh, not a tourist-adjusted version of it. It's included in your stay and adjusted for dietary needs with advance notice.

Bottom line: a Kashmir trip without a real houseboat night is an incomplete trip. If you're staying on Dal Lake anyway, the difference between an average houseboat and Olympias is the difference between checking a box and having the memory people ask about for years.
