Guest Story · First Stay

My First Night on Dal Lake

An honest account of arriving at Olympias for the very first time — and why it stopped feeling like a photograph.

I'd seen the photos a dozen times before booking — the carved walnut walls, the sundeck with its view of the Zabarwan range — and I fully expected the reality to fall a little short, the way it usually does. It didn't.

The Shikara picked us up right at the ghat as promised, no waiting around, no confusion about which boat was ours. The boatman knew Saif by first name, which told me something before I'd even stepped aboard Olympias. Ten quiet minutes across the water, past floating gardens and a few other houseboats lit up for the evening, and there it was — smaller and warmer-looking in person than in any photo, lamplight coming through carved wooden lattice windows.

"Saif met us at the door himself — not a manager, not a receptionist, the actual owner — and the first thing he asked wasn't about payment. It was whether we'd eaten."

The room itself is where the photographs undersell things. Walnut carving isn't a decorative accent here, it's everywhere — the ceiling, the bed frame, the window screens — done by hand, and you can tell. Nothing about it feels staged for Instagram. It feels like a room that's been cared for by the same family for a long time, because it has.

Dinner was Wazwan, cooked that evening, not reheated — Rogan Josh with the right amount of Kashmiri chili, and a dum aloo that I'm still thinking about weeks later. We ate on the sundeck as the lake went from gold to black, and Saif sat with us for a while afterward, not selling anything, just talking about the lake, his family, the winters here.

I've stayed in a lot of "boutique" places that trade on authenticity as a marketing word. This was the first time in a while that the word actually applied. If you're on the fence about a first Dal Lake houseboat stay, this is the version of it that doesn't disappoint once you're actually there.
