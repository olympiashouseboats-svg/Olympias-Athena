Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden: Asia's Largest Tulip Display

The Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden in Srinagar is Asia's largest tulip garden and one of Kashmir's most spectacular spring attractions. Spread across 74 acres on the foothills of Zabarwan mountains overlooking Dal Lake, this terraced garden showcases over 1.5 million tulips in 68 varieties creating a riot of colors every April.

Opened in 2007, the Tulip Garden has become the centerpiece of Srinagar's annual Tulip Festival, attracting hundreds of thousands of visitors from across India and the world during its brief two-week blooming period.

What to Expect at Tulip Garden

68 Tulip Varieties

The garden showcases 68 different tulip varieties imported from Netherlands, including exotic varieties in colors ranging from pure white and yellow to deep purple, pink, red, and multicolored blooms. Each variety is labeled with its name and origin.

Seven Terraces

The garden is laid out in seven terraces ascending the hillside, each terrace featuring different tulip varieties and color schemes. Walking paths wind through the terraces offering stunning views of colorful tulip beds against Dal Lake and mountain backdrops.

Cultural Programs

During the festival, the garden hosts Kashmiri cultural programs including music performances, traditional dance, handicraft exhibitions, and food stalls offering local delicacies.

Photography Tips

  • Golden Hour: Early morning (9-10 AM) light is best for photography
  • Avoid Crowds: Weekdays have fewer visitors obstructing shots
  • Bring Wide-Angle Lens: Capture expansive tulip fields and mountain backgrounds
  • Focus on Details: Close-ups of individual tulip blooms are stunning
  • Use Foreground: Frame shots with tulips in foreground and Dal Lake in background
