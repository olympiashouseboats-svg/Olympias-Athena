Sonamarg: The Meadow of Gold

Sonamarg, literally meaning "Meadow of Gold" (Sona = Gold, Marg = Meadow), is Kashmir's most dramatic and breathtaking hill station. Located 87 km northeast of Srinagar at an elevation of 2,800 meters (9,186 feet) in the Ganderbal district, Sonamarg sits along the banks of the Sindh River and serves as the last major town before the legendary Zojila Pass leading into Ladakh.

The name "Meadow of Gold" comes from the golden wildflowers — particularly saffron and other alpine flowers — that blanket the valley during summer months. Sonamarg offers some of Kashmir's most dramatic scenery: towering glaciers visible from the road, rushing mountain rivers, vast alpine meadows, and the majestic Himalayas creating an unforgettable backdrop.

Top Attractions in Sonamarg

Thajiwas Glacier

The most famous attraction of Sonamarg, Thajiwas Glacier is a massive glacier visible from Sonamarg town, accessible by a 2 km trek or horse ride (₹400-600). The glacier remains snow-covered even in peak summer, offering sledging and snow play activities. The trek through pine forests to reach the glacier base is itself a memorable experience. Best visited: May-September.

Vishansar and Krishansar Lakes

Two stunning alpine lakes accessible by the famous Sonamarg-Vishansar Trek (3-4 days). Vishansar Lake (3,710 meters) and Krishansar Lake nearby are high-altitude glacial lakes surrounded by wildflower meadows and snow peaks. The lakes are sacred in Hindu mythology and offer spectacular photography opportunities.

Nilagrad River

Famous for its rich trout fishing, Nilagrad River flows near Sonamarg offering angling opportunities in one of Kashmir's most scenic river settings. The river is crystal clear, fed by glacial meltwater, and surrounded by alpine vegetation. Fishing permit required (₹200/day from J&K Tourism).

Zojila Pass (Zero Point)

The dramatic mountain pass at 3,528 meters connecting Kashmir to Ladakh. The road to Zero Point (the last motorable point near Sonamarg before the pass becomes inaccessible) offers incredible high-altitude scenery with snow walls up to 20 feet high even in July. Day trips to Zero Point are extremely popular. Only open June-October.

Gadsar Lake

Remote high-altitude lake accessible via the Great Lakes Trek from Sonamarg. Surrounded by towering peaks and wildflower meadows, Gadsar is one of Kashmir's most beautiful but least visited lakes. Requires 4-5 days trekking and good physical fitness.

Sindh River

The mighty Sindh River rushes through Sonamarg creating opportunities for trout fishing and enjoying the dramatic mountain river scenery. Riverside camping along the Sindh is particularly popular with adventure travelers.

Adventure Activities in Sonamarg

  • Glacier Trekking: Thajiwas Glacier day trek (2-3 hours, easy-moderate)
  • Great Lakes Trek: 7-day epic trek through 7 high-altitude lakes (requires guide)
  • Snow Activities: Sledging, snow tubing at Thajiwas (May-July)
  • Trout Fishing: Nilagrad and Sindh rivers (permit required)
  • Horse Riding: To Thajiwas and surrounding meadows
  • Camping: Riverside and meadow camping
  • Mountain Biking: Trails along Sindh River valley

Best Time to Visit Sonamarg

May-June: Best time. Snow activities at glacier, alpine flowers blooming, all roads open

July-August: Wildflowers peak, Zojila Pass open, excellent trekking

September-October: Autumn foliage, fewer crowds, clear skies

November-April: Road often closed; Sonamarg inaccessible in deep winter

Sonamarg from Srinagar

Distance: 87 km from Srinagar

Travel Time: 2.5-3 hours by road

Route: Srinagar → Kangan → Gagangir → Sonamarg (NH1)

Taxi: ₹2,500-3,500 round trip from Srinagar

Travel Tips for Sonamarg

  • Altitude Awareness: Sonamarg is at 2,800m — take it easy on first day
  • Warm Clothing: Essential even in July; temperatures drop dramatically in evenings
  • Start Early: Leave Srinagar by 7 AM to make most of the day
  • Glacier Fees: Entry fees at Thajiwas Glacier (₹50-100)
  • Inner Line Permit: Not required for Sonamarg; required for Zojila Pass
  • Photography: Extra batteries essential in cold temperatures
