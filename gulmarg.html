Gulmarg: Asia's Skiing Capital and Meadow of Flowers

Gulmarg, meaning "Meadow of Flowers," is Kashmir's most famous ski resort and one of India's premier tourist destinations. Located 56 km from Srinagar in the Baramulla district at 2,650 meters elevation, Gulmarg offers a unique combination of summer attractions (alpine meadows, wildflowers, golf, trekking) and world-class winter skiing that attracts visitors from across the globe.

Gulmarg holds the distinction of having Asia's highest gondola cable car, which takes visitors to Apharwat Peak at 4,390 meters offering panoramic views of the Himalayas including Nanga Parbat — one of the world's most dramatic mountain vistas. In winter, Gulmarg transforms into India's best ski destination with powder snow and challenging runs.

Top Attractions in Gulmarg

Gulmarg Gondola (Ropeway)

Asia's highest gondola operating in two phases. Phase 1 (2,650m to 3,088m): Spectacular views of Gulmarg bowl and surrounding peaks. Phase 2 (3,088m to 3,980m): Takes you to Apharwat Peak with breathtaking 360-degree Himalayan panorama including views of Nanga Parbat (8,126m). Tickets: ₹900 Phase 1, ₹1,600 both phases. Advance booking essential in peak season.

Apharwat Peak

At 4,390 meters, Apharwat Peak is accessible via Gondola Phase 2. The peak offers some of the most dramatic mountain views in India, with unobstructed sightlines to Pakistan's Nanga Parbat, multiple glaciers, and the vast Himalayan landscape. Snow remains year-round at the peak.

Gulmarg Golf Course

The Gulmarg Golf Club, established in 1891 by the British, is one of the world's highest golf courses at 2,650 meters and the oldest in Asia. The 18-hole course offers spectacular mountain backdrop. Open May-October. Green fees: ₹500-1,500. Club rental available.

Gulmarg Biosphere Reserve

Dense forests of fir, pine, and deodar surrounding Gulmarg protect numerous wildlife species including Himalayan black bear, Kashmir stag (hangul), musk deer, and diverse bird species. Forest walks offer wildlife spotting opportunities in this protected reserve.

St. Mary's Church

A picturesque colonial-era church built in 1902, St. Mary's adds historic charm to Gulmarg's landscape. The church is no longer in active use but its stone architecture against mountain backgrounds creates excellent photography opportunities.

Maharani Temple

A hilltop Shiva temple built by Maharani Moksha Parvati Bai offering panoramic views of Gulmarg valley and surrounding peaks. The hike to the temple through forests takes 30-45 minutes and is worth every step for the views at the top.

Activities in Gulmarg

Winter (December-March):

  • Skiing: India's best skiing with slopes for beginners to advanced (equipment rental available)
  • Snowboarding: Growing scene with dedicated terrain
  • Heli-Skiing: Premium helicopter skiing on virgin powder (₹25,000+ per run)
  • Snow Tubing & Sledging: Family-friendly snow activities
  • Snowmobiling: Tours across snowy meadows

Summer (May-October):

  • Gondola Rides: Both phases for mountain views
  • Golf: 18-hole course at 2,650m
  • Trekking: Trails to Alpather Lake, Frozen Lake, Ningle Nallah
  • Horse Riding: Through meadows and forests
  • Mountain Biking: Forest trails
  • Photography: Wildflower meadows, Himalayan panoramas

Best Time to Visit Gulmarg

December-March (Skiing Season): Heavy snowfall, world-class skiing, frozen gondola lake, magical winter scenery

April-May: Snow melting, spring flowers emerging, fewer crowds

June-August: Peak summer season, full wildflower bloom, all activities open

September-November: Autumn colors, clear skies, excellent trekking

Travel Tips for Gulmarg

  • Gondola Tickets: Book online in advance during peak season to avoid long queues
  • Weather: Gulmarg weather changes rapidly; carry rain gear and warm layers
  • Altitude: Take it slow initially; altitude sickness rare but possible above 3,000m
  • Ski Equipment: Rental available in Gulmarg town; quality varies so inspect before taking
  • Food: Limited quality restaurants; carry snacks for Gondola rides
  • Stay: Day trip from Srinagar is practical; Gulmarg hotels are expensive
